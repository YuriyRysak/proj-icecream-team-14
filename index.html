How it’s made?

Special techniques are employed to make ice cream that creates smaller ice crystals and incorporates air, which produces a soft texture. Constantly churning ice cream, whether by hand or mechanically, ensures that large, stiff ice crystals do not form within the mixture. The churning process also serves to introduce air and create a foam-like texture, further softening the mixture.
